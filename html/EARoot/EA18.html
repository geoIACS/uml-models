<br/>--<b>Name</b>--<br/>Ecological focus area type value<br/><br/>-- <b>Definition </b>--<br/>Types of EFA as listed in Art. 46(2) of (EU) R 1307/2013.<br/><br/>-- <b>Description </b>--<br/>MS shall decide that one or more values of this code list  are considered to be ecological focus area.<br/><br/>NOTE 1: The extension of this code list shall be documented in the eligibility profile of the MS/region in a register / registry service to publish the extended values and their definitions.<br/>NOTE 2: This concept is relevant only for period covered by CAP before 2023.<br/>

Notes: <br/>--<b>Name</b>--<br/>Buffer strips<br/><br/>-- <b>Definition </b>--<br/>Ecological focus area type as defined by Art. 46(2) of (EU) R 1307/2013.<br/><br/>-- <b>Description </b>--<br/>Buffer strips, including buffer strips covered by permanent grassland, provided that these are distinct from adjacent eligible agricultural area [Art. 46(2) (d) of (EU) R 1307/2013].<br/><br/>NOTE 1: Buffer strips shall include the buffer strips:<br/>- along water courses required under GAEC 1, SMR 1 or SMR 10 as referred to in Annex II to Regulation (EU) No 640/2014;<br/>- other buffer strips defined by MS. minimum width that shall not be below 1 meter.<br/><br/>NOTE 2: Buffer strips shall be located on or adjacent to an arable field with their long edges parallel to the edge of a water course or water body.<br/><br/>NOTE 3:  Along water courses, buffer strips may include strips with riparian vegetation with a width of up to 10 meters with no agricultural production. (By way of derogation MS may allow grazing or cutting, provided that the buffer strip remains distinguishable from adjacent agricultural land).<br/>
